How Nike Began

Nike started in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports, founded by Phil Knight and Bill Bowerman. The company began as a distributor for Onitsuka Tiger, a Japanese shoe brand (now known as ASICS).

In 1971, they launched their own line of shoes under a new name — Nike, inspired by the Greek goddess of victory. The now-iconic Swoosh logo was designed by graphic design student Carolyn Davidson for just $35.
